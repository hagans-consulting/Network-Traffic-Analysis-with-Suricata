SECTION 3: SHORT ANSWER & SCENARIO
-----------------------------------------------------------------------------

7. Scenario Answer:
   Two possible configuration errors:
   1. Incorrect HOME_NET: If the scanner's IP or target IP is not included in the HOME_NET variable, Suricata may not apply the relevant rules.
   2. Wrong Interface: Suricata might be listening on the wrong network interface (e.g., `eth0` instead of `wlp2s0`), meaning it never sees the traffic.
   (Other valid answers: Rules not updated, Suricata service not running).

8. Policy & Risk Answer:
   Running IDS tools and generating scan traffic on corporate/public networks is discouraged because:
   1. Policy Violation: It often violates Acceptable Use Policies (AUP) regarding network scanning and unauthorized security testing.
   2. False Positives: It can trigger the organization's own security alerts (SIEM/SOC), wasting their time and potentially getting you flagged as an insider threat.
   3. Network Disruption: Aggressive scanning can impact network performance or crash sensitive IoT devices on the shared network.
